The most common species include Yellow Stem Borer (Scirpophaga incertulas) and White Stem Borer (Scirpophaga innotata). These pests affect rice at various stages of its growth.
Symptoms are: Dead Heart (at the vegetative stage),Visible signs,White Ear Head/Panicle (at the reproductive stage) .to prevent this use Cultural Control, Biological Control,Pheromone Traps etc.

The Rice Stem Borer (Scirpophaga incertulas), also known as the Yellow Stem Borer, primarily causes physical damage to rice plants rather than transmitting diseases. It does not directly cause diseases like a virus or fungus might, but the damage it causes leads to significant crop loss and weakens the plants, making them more susceptible to secondary infections and stress.
The symptoms caused by the Yellow Stem Borer (Scirpophaga incertulas) in rice plants are:Dead Heart (Vegetative Stage), White Ear Heads (Reproductive Stage) etc.To prevent this we use Cultural Control(Synchronized Planting,Crop Rotation etc).

The Green Leafhopper (Nephotettix virescens) is a major pest in rice crops and is known to be the primary vector for several viral diseases.
Symptoms of Disease are:Rice Tungro Disease,Rice Grassy Stunt,Rice Yellow Dwarf. To prevent this we useCultural Control, Biological Control,Mechanical Control,Chemical Control.

Rice Gall Midge (Orseolia oryzae) is a major pest in rice cultivation, known for causing significant yield losses. The pest does not transmit viral or bacterial diseases but rather causes physical damage to the rice plant, leading to abnormal growth. This pest is responsible for inducing a condition called "Silver Shoot" or "Onion Shoot", which severely hampers the plant’s development
Symptoms of Gall Midge Infestation are:Silver Shoot (Vegetative Stage) .Prevention and Control Measures are:Cultural Control, Biological Control,Mechanical Control,Pheromone Traps etc.
.

Leaf Roller (Cnaphalocrocis medinalis) is a common pest in rice fields that causes significant damage by feeding on the leaves of rice plants. The larvae of the Leaf Roller roll the leaves and feed inside, reducing the plant’s ability to photosynthesize effectively. While the Leaf Roller does not directly cause viral or bacterial diseases, its feeding behavior results in physical damage that can severely impact the health of the rice plant.
Symptoms are:Rolled Leaves,Leaf Tunneling and Skeletonization,Yellowing of Leaves etc. to prevent this we use:Cultural Control,Biological Control,Mechanical Control,Chemical Control etc.
