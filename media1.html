Brown spot, caused by the fungus Helminthosporium oryzae (also known as Cochliobolus miyabeanus), primarily affects rice plants but can also attack other cereals. The disease causes small, round to oval brown lesions on the leaves, leaf sheaths, stems, and grains. Over time, these lesions may expand, leading to a "blighted" appearance of the leaves.
symptoms of this are: Leaf Spots,Grain Infection ,Reduced Photosynthesis,Pre-harvest Yield Loss.

Sheath blight, caused by the fungus Rhizoctonia solani, primarily affects rice plants, but can also impact other crops like soybeans and vegetables. The disease manifests as water-soaked lesions on the leaf sheaths, which can coalesce into large, irregular patches. As the disease progresses, these patches can turn brown and dry out, leading to reduced photosynthesis and lower yields.
To prevent Rhizoctonia solani (Sheath Blight) in plants, we Use disease-resistant or tolerant rice varieties,Remove plant debris and weeds to reduce fungal sources,Avoid waterlogging and ensure proper drainage in fields, Ensure adequate spacing to promote air circulation and reduce humidity.

Rice Leaf Curl Virus (RLCV) is a significant disease that affects rice crops. When rice plants are infected by a leaf curl virus, they can show symptoms that affect plant growth and yield.
symptoms are:Leaf curling,Stunting,Chlorosis,Tillering reduction.

Preventing the Rice Leaf Curl Virus (RLCV) and other similar viral diseases in rice largely we have to use Plant virus-resistant rice varieties,Biological control(Predators,Parasitoids), Chemical control etc.
